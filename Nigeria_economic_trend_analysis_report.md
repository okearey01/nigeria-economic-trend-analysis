 ## Introduction

This report presents an analysis of Nigeria’s economic trends from 1990 to 2022. The analysis focuses on key macroeconomic indicators such as GDP, inflation, unemployment, government debt, and sector contributions to understand how they influence economic performance over time.

## Project Description

This project focuses on analyzing Nigeria’s economic trends over time. It explores how key economic indicators such as GDP, inflation, unemployment, government debt, and sector contributions reflect the performance of the Nigerian economy from 1990 to 2022.

## Project Aim

The aim of this project is to answer the following questions:

- What is the relationship between inflation and GDP?
- Which years experienced economic growth or decline?
- Which sector contributes most to Nigeria’s GDP?
- What defines a stable economic year?
- How does government debt behave over time?

  ## About the Dataset

The dataset contains 34 rows and 9 columns representing annual economic data for Nigeria from 1990 to 2022.

Columns include:
- GDP (Nominal/Real)
- Inflation Rate
- Unemployment Rate
- Government Debt (% of GDP)
- Agriculture Contribution to GDP
- Industry Contribution to GDP
- Services Contribution to GDP
- GDP Growth Rate
- Year
 

  ## Data Cleaning & Preparation

  ## Insights from Data Analysis

### 1. Inflation vs GDP Relationship
Inflation shows a mostly inverse relationship with GDP. Higher inflation periods often align with lower GDP performance.

### 2. Economic Growth and Decline
The economy experienced significant fluctuations, with notable decline in 1995 followed by sharp recovery in 1996.

### 3. Sector Contribution
The services sector contributes the highest share to Nigeria’s GDP, followed by agriculture and industry.

### 4. Stable Economic Year
A stable economic year is defined as a year with high GDP, low inflation, and controlled government debt.

Based on this definition, 2007 is identified as the most stable economic year.

### 5. Government Debt Insight
Government debt fluctuations between 1992–1995 coincided with economic instability and high inflation, suggesting a possible link between debt management and macroeconomic performance.


## Recommendations

- Implement policies to control inflation and stabilize the economy
- Diversify the economy by strengthening agriculture and industry
- Reduce overdependence on the services sector
- Adopt gradual and controlled debt management strategies


  ## Conclusion

This analysis shows that inflation, GDP, and government debt play significant roles in Nigeria’s economic performance.

Economic stability is achieved when inflation is controlled, GDP is strong, and government debt is managed sustainably.
